Earthquake in Indonesia

  • Posted on 7 May, 2021
  • Bulletin
  • By Rora

“A 6.2 magnitude earthquake attacked the western coast of Sulawesi island in Indonesia early Friday, killing 46 people, destroying their houses, flattening the hospitals and setting off landslides. The rescuers were searching for people trapped in the wreckage. More than 600 people were injured, which destroy inland between the coastal cities of Mamuju and Majene. No Tsunami warning was issued.

Ardiansyah, an emergency response official for West Sulawesi Province said that he was afraid of how many fatalities. The National Disaster Mitigation Agency said Saturday that at least 46 people were killed by the quake. Most of the fatalities took place in Mamuju, the larger of the two coastal cities. They were still evacuating and erecting shelters. Many people were buried under the ruins.

The area hit by the quake was about 160 miles southwest of the city of Palu, where a destructive and tsunami attacked in 2018, destroyed part of the city and killing thousands of people. Disaster officials said they expected the number of deaths and injuries from the quake on Friday to rising as they received information from areas that had been cut off. One bridge was destroyed, roads were damaged and communications were limited. The governor’s office in Mamuju was also damaged.

Indonesia Disaster Mitigation Agency released the video that shows a girl identified only an Angel trapped in the ruins of her family’s home. Only her face was seen through a gap in the wreckage. At least 3 others were trapped in the house with her, as they said.

In that video, she tells rescuers that she can hear the voice of another girl who is trapped nearby and cannot move.

In Mamuju, Mitra Hospital collapsed. Officials said 5 nurses and patients were trapped inside. The government hospital was heavily damaged. The flight control tower at Mamuju’s commercial airport was damaged, and flight control duties were taken over by the air navigation office in Makassar, south of Mamuju

The authorities warned the public to avoid buildings because of the possibility of another strong earthquake. Six temblors of magnitude 2.9 and above were recorded 12 hours before the major quake struck at 2.28 a.m. local time. Nine aftershocks were recorded in the hours that followed.”
